📼 PW Short Form Video Editing Guidelines

authors: @Seyi Olujide @Layomi Kupoluyi, @You?

To ensure our videos stay impactful and optimized for engagement, follow these principles during the editing process, before sending in an edit for review make sure it passes all these checks:

1. ⏱ Keep It Short
Aim for videos under 60 seconds.
The shorter the better.
Videos longer than 90 seconds tend to underperform or get demonetized on platforms like YouTube.

2. ⚡ First 3 Seconds Matter
Avoid fade-ins, blur-ins, or slow intros.
Start immediately with the most compelling moment of the video.
Hook the viewer within the first 3-10 seconds.

3. ✂️ Snappy Pacing
Use jump cuts to remove pauses, filler words, or dead space.
The video should feel tight and energetic at all times.
Keep the viewer engaged from start to finish.

4. 🎥 Movement & Visual Energy
Videos should have a sense of motion—avoid stagnant or lifeless visuals.
If using a tripod or static shot, add movement in post (e.g., dynamic zoom in/out, slow pans) to keep the visual energy alive.
For handheld footage that feels too shaky, apply stabilization during editing to maintain a professional and watchable feel.
The goal is to immerse the viewer and create a flow that feels intentional and alive—movement helps tell the story.

5. ✍️ Subtitles & On-Screen Text
Always include subtitles for dialogue or lyrics.
We recommend using the Captions app but any other subtitle app will work:
Font: "Reel" (On Captions app) or "SF Pro Bold" Everywhere else - download
Font size: relatively small (see example)
Font color: Yellow #eaca6d or White #ffffff
🔕 No profanity is allowed. Use your judgment.
If in doubt, check with the team.
For sensitive words (e.g., "r*pe," "ab*se," "k*ll myself"):
Mute the word in audio.
Censor it in the subtitle if essential to the message.

6. 🧠 On-Screen Captions
These are short, engaging headlines that appear during the first 5 seconds.
Used to hook viewers visually and quickly convey the video's focus.
✅ Created after the first edit by the writing team.
Do not include them unless specifically requested.

7. 🎵 Music & Audio
Always choose music that fits the tone and emotional arc of the video. The soundtrack should enhance the message, not distract from it.
Try to add in background sounds such as park sounds, city sounds etc. You can always find these on YouTube or find them in the Resources/Sound Effects Folder
Be on the lookout for trending sounds that can help boost engagement, especially for platforms like Instagram Reels and TikTok.
However, do not use trending sounds where the original audio or context is antithetical to Christian values (e.g., lyrics with profanity, sexual content, or occult references).
When in doubt, do not use it—reach out to the team to confirm.
Editors are encouraged to pull from the Audio Edits folder, which includes pre-cleared music created for Public Worship content.
If you're unsure what fits, the music team is available to help select or create the right audio track.
